Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Portland Hacker Foundation?

The Portland Hacker Foundation is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization with a mission to provide grants to people or organizations in the community who are building great things, with a strong focus on information security and related fields.

How do you decide who gets grants?

Grant decisions are made through a voting process. We have an annual voting ceremony where our community and sponsors can vote on which projects to fund. Projects are evaluated based on their potential for asymmetric impact, meaning small amounts of money can enable significant work.

How can I apply for a grant?

You can apply for a grant by filling out our grant application form. We're looking for projects that align with our mission and values, especially those that can have a significant impact with modest funding.

What kinds of projects do you fund?

We fund a wide range of projects in information security and related fields. Early on, we're particularly focused on university research projects, but we're open to creative work, educational initiatives, and technical innovations that align with hacker ethos.

How can I become a sponsor?

You can become a sponsor by making a donation through our PayPal donation link. We offer sponsorship at various levels: $10/year, $100/year, and $1,000/year, each with different voting weights for the grant selection process.

Are donations tax-deductible?

Yes, as a registered 501(c)(3) organization, donations to the Portland Hacker Foundation are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.

What is HACKLUNCH?

HACKLUNCH is our monthly gathering where community members meet to discuss foundation-related topics, share ideas, and network. Initially, these lunches are paid for individually by attendees.

How is the foundation's money managed?

Donations are stored in a treasury consisting of low-risk index funds. Each year, we allocate up to 5% for grants, up to 2% for events, and up to 2% for operational costs. Our goal is to grow the treasury over time to enable more community support.
